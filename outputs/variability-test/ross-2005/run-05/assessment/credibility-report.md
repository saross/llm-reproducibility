## Contextual Interpretation

This section explains what scores **mean for this specific paper type**, helping readers understand why certain scores may differ from typical empirical research expectations.

This paper is interpretive philology with no computational component. Scores should be interpreted within the context of humanities scholarship rather than scientific reproducibility standards:

- **Moderate Transparency (55)** reflects disciplinary conventions for classical philology in 2005, where methodology is typically embedded in argument rather than explicitly declared
- **Moderate Robustness (50)** is characteristic of abductive/interpretive research that argues for best explanation rather than exhaustively testing alternatives
- **Moderate Reproducibility (55)** uses the methodological transparency variant ‚Äî the question is not "can we rerun analysis" but "can another scholar access sources and follow reasoning"

### Signal-Specific Context

#### Robustness (50, Moderate)

**Why this score:** Abductive/interpretive research infers the best explanation rather than systematically testing all alternatives. A Moderate Robustness score reflects the inherent nature of philological argument, not methodological weakness.

**What this means:** The paper builds a coherent case for proto-Panhellenism through careful textual analysis. Some triangulation is present (intertextual comparison across four works), but exhaustive comparison of alternative theoretical frameworks is not provided.

**What readers should consider:** Engage critically with the oral tradition theory framework that grounds the interpretation. Consider how alternative dating hypotheses or neo-analytical approaches might affect conclusions.

#### Reproducibility (55, Moderate) üîß

**Why this score:** This assessment uses the methodological transparency variant because no computational workflow exists to reproduce. A score of 55 indicates that methods are identifiable but procedures are implicit; an experienced classicist could apply a similar approach but would generate their own interpretive conclusions.

**What this means:** "Reproduction" for this paper means independent scholarly engagement with the same evidence and theoretical framework. The interpretive approach is transparent enough to critique but not precise enough to replicate exactly.

**What is reproducible:** Verification of textual evidence (all passages independently checkable), engagement with theoretical framework (well-documented oral tradition theory), critique of interpretive logic (traceable argument structure).

**What is not reproducible:** Identical interpretive conclusions (inherently subjective element in philological analysis), tacit knowledge of an experienced classicist.
