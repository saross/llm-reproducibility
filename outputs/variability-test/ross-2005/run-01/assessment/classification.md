## Paper Type Classification

### Primary Type: Empirical

**Rationale:** This paper analyses empirical evidence from primary sources (ancient Greek texts) to support claims about historical phenomena (proto-Panhellenism in eighth-century BCE Greece). While interpretive rather than quantitative, the paper makes claims about historical reality based on textual evidence.

**Subtype:** Interpretive Philology

The paper uses close reading and comparative analysis of ancient texts to reconstruct historical attitudes and identities. It examines linguistic patterns across Homer's Iliad, Odyssey, Hesiod's Theogony, and Homeric Hymns.

## Research Approach Classification

### Primary Approach: Abductive (Inference to Best Explanation)

**Confidence:** High

**Evidence for Classification:**

1. **Explanatory Structure:** The paper proposes that the observed pattern of linguistic representation (diversity among Trojan allies, uniformity among Akhaians) is best explained by a nascent proto-Panhellenism in eighth-century BCE Greece.

2. **Key Quote (C001):**
   > "Even though no language barrier separates Akhaians from Trojans in the epics, the selective recognition of linguistic diversity among Trojan epikouroi, versus the homogeneity of the Akhaians, offers a glimpse of an undeveloped and unstable proto-Panhellenism."

3. **Alternative Explanations Considered:** The author considers and addresses alternative scholarly interpretations:
   - Opposition-based Panhellenism (rejected as anachronistic for eighth century)
   - Aggregative Panhellenism (accepted, with linguistic component added)
   - Literary convention vs historical reality (addressed via Mackie)

4. **Theoretical Framework:** Adopts Nagy's oral tradition theory to justify dating conclusions to the period of epic stabilisation (~700 BCE).

**Abductive Reasoning Pattern:**
- **Observation:** Three Iliad passages show linguistic diversity among Trojan allies but none among Akhaians
- **Puzzle:** Why this asymmetric treatment?
- **Best Explanation:** Reflects emerging proto-Panhellenic identity based on linguistic unity
- **Supporting Inference:** Pattern resonated with eighth-century audiences due to nascent Panhellenism

### Secondary Approach: Not Applicable

The paper does not employ a secondary research approach. It is purely abductive/interpretive.

## Classification Rationale Summary

This classical philology paper uses abductive reasoning to explain patterns in Homer's representation of linguistic diversity. The author observes a consistent asymmetry (diversity among Trojan allies, uniformity among Akhaians) and proposes proto-Panhellenism as the best explanation, grounded in Nagy's oral tradition dating framework. The approach is empirical-interpretive rather than hypothetico-deductive: the author does not test pre-specified hypotheses but rather builds an explanatory case from accumulated textual evidence.

The high classification confidence reflects the paper's clear argumentative structure and explicit engagement with alternative scholarly positions.
