## TL;DR

The LLM-era protocol/methods-extraction literature that lives on arXiv is dominated by a **chemistry/materials synthesis-procedure lineage** (ChemCrow descendants: action/event extraction, provenance graphs, procedure generation) plus a fast-growing **reproducibility/replication-agent** strand and a distinct **LLM-assisted qualitative-methods** cluster in the social sciences — but almost nothing sits at the *appropriateness-judgement* layer, and there is a hard gap: **zero preprints apply methods/protocol extraction to archaeology or fieldwork**. Top-3 must-reads for a three-tier explicit+implicit extraction-plus-appropriateness project: **Laï-king & Paroubek (2025)** on LLM scoring of clinical-trial reporting *quality* (`10.48550/arXiv.2510.04338`, the appropriateness half), **Carriero et al. (2024)** on procedural knowledge-graph extraction with a human-evaluation protocol (`10.48550/arXiv.2412.03589`, the protocols tier, published at EKAW 2025), and **Schmidt et al. (2024)** on the empirical error modes of LLM methods-field extraction from papers (`10.48550/arXiv.2405.14445`, 33 cites). Biggest gap: no preprint treats *implicit* (unreported-but-deducible) methodological inference as a first-class task outside wet-lab reproduction, and none touch HASS/archaeological fieldwork — a genuinely open niche for the user's contribution.

## Landscape

The published DOI-grounded sweep already mapped the *formally published* methods-extraction canon (Menke 2025, SciER/SciERC, RobotReviewer, CONSORT-NLP, AutoReporter, statcheck/SciScore, Tan & D'Souza 2026, Simmons et al. 2025). The arXiv layer beneath it has a different centre of gravity. Five observations:

1. **The synthesis-procedure lineage is the mature core.** The single densest, oldest, and most methodologically developed strand is chemistry/materials synthesis-procedure extraction (Cluster A, 8 rows) — a direct descendant of the Walker et al. (2023) GPT-3 nanorod work and the ChemCrow/Chemputer tradition. It has moved from flat entity/action extraction (ChemActor) to **provenance graphs** (MatPROV) and **generative procedure synthesis** (Liu et al. 2025), and even to *executable validation* of literature protocols (Pagel et al. 2024, "chemputation"). This is the most transferable engineering, but it is domain-narrow and assumes highly regular procedural prose — the opposite of narrative fieldwork.

2. **Reproducibility/replication agents are the fastest-growing strand (2025–2026)** and the one that most explicitly forces *implicit*-methods inference: to reproduce a study an agent must recover unstated design and parameter choices (Dobbins et al. 2025; R-LAM; CodeScientist). ReplicatorBench (already in your library) is the HASS-facing anchor here.

3. **Methods-quality / appropriateness judgement is thin.** Only Laï-king & Paroubek (2025) and, adjacently, CheckSupport (reporting-checklist completion) actually *score* reporting quality/completeness rather than merely extracting fields. This is precisely the layer your project's "appropriateness" deliverable occupies — and it is under-served on arXiv.

4. **A separate HASS/qualitative-methods conversation exists but does not talk to the extraction literature.** Cluster F (5 rows: thematic saturation, axial coding, deductive content analysis, theory-driven qualitative analysis) is about LLMs *doing* qualitative method, not *extracting and appraising* method from papers. It is the nearest disciplinary neighbour to your work sociologically, yet methodologically orthogonal — a bridge worth building.

5. **Code availability was not systematically verified this run** (I prioritised metadata grounding).
