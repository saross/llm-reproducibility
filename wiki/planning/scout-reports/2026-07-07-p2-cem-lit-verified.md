## Landscape

**Overall shape.** Your six sub-areas are real and mostly distinct communities, but they publish in different venues and at different maturities:

1. **Scientific claim extraction & verification (SciFact lineage)** — a mature, benchmark-driven NLP community centred on ACL-family venues. SciFact (#1) spawned MultiVerS (#2), SciFact-Open (#3), and a continuing stream of verification/evidence-retrieval work (#4, #11). The task is *verification against a corpus*, not *decomposition of a paper's own argument* — an important framing difference from CEM.
2. **Argument mining on scholarly full text** — smaller but directly on-point, clustered around the SDP and ArgMining workshops with occasional journal outputs (TACL #6, Scientometrics #8). Al Khatib (#5) is the field's stock-taking statement; Binder (#7) is the clearest full-text task; PaperTrail (#10) is the freshest LLM-era, claim–evidence-interface instantiation. This is the sub-area your explicit-claims pass competes in most directly.
3. **Implicit / unstated claims & premises** — genuinely sparse. The literature is small (Green #13, Ku #14) with a just-published survey (#15). This is under-served and is where your implicit-claims pass is most differentiating.
4. **Structured scholarly knowledge extraction into graphs** — a Semantic-Web/e-Science lineage (Micropublications #16, nanopublications #20, ORKG #17–18) now meeting LLM extraction (#21). Rich on *representation*, comparatively thin on *automated, whole-paper claim+evidence population from full text*.
5. **LLM extraction for systematic reviews / living evidence synthesis** — the most crowded and fastest-growing area, overwhelmingly in medical-informatics journals (JAMIA, JMIR, Annals, npj Digital Medicine, J Clin Epi). Khraisha (#22) is the citation anchor; a dozen 2024–2025 papers (#23–27, #35) already benchmark extraction accuracy vs humans.
